Your Facebook Page Is Not Enough — Here's Why Your Business Needs a Real Website

For sellers, professionals, and business owners relying on Facebook — this is the honest answer to the question: "Do I really need a website?"

Web Developer · czerwin.net

You know how this goes. You started your business on Facebook — posting products, sharing updates, managing orders through Messenger. It worked at first. But at some point, the DMs became a nightmare.

That's what happens when Facebook is your entire business platform. It's not a path to growth — it's a path to burnout.

Why a Facebook Page Is No Longer Enough

We're not saying Facebook is useless. It's still a great tool for reaching people. But running your entire business inside Facebook — orders, payments, customer service, product catalog — that's the problem.

1. You don't own it

Your Facebook page? It belongs to Facebook. They can restrict your reach without warning. Your account can disappear overnight — and take all your followers with it. With your own website, you're in control.

2. The algorithm controls you

You post a product. Two people see it. Why? Because the algorithm isn't in your favor today. With your own website, when a customer searches Google for "flower shop Quezon City" — you show up, not someone else's Facebook feed.

3. No system — pure chaos

DMs for orders. GCash screenshots for payment. Manual tracking of who paid and who didn't. How long can you sustain that? With even a basic website, everything becomes automated — order confirmation, payment, tracking.

4. You don't look serious

It's true. Telling a potential client "just message me on Facebook" versus "visit us at yourname.com" are two completely different levels of credibility. Especially for professionals — doctors, lawyers, architects, consultants.

⚡ Harsh truth When a potential customer Googles you and no website comes up — for them, it's like you don't officially exist. Just a Facebook profile? They'll wonder: "Is this even legit?"

How to Get Started — Step by Step

1

Figure out what kind of website you need

Business site? Online store? Portfolio? Booking page? The right format depends on your industry and goals.

2

Get a domain name (e.g., yourbusiness.com)

This is your website's address. Roughly ₱800–₱1,200 per year. Essential for credibility and SEO.

3

Avoid free website builders

Wix, Weebly, and similar platforms have the same problem as Facebook — you don't truly own what you build. A properly hosted website is always better in the long run.

4

Work with a developer who understands the local market

GCash? COD? J&T? LBC? It matters that your developer actually knows how business works in the Philippines — not just someone copying a foreign template.

5

Link your website across all your social media

Keep using Facebook — but as a traffic source pointing to your website, not as your primary business platform.

You Don't Have to Abandon Facebook

The right mindset is this: use Facebook to reach people, but use your website to run your business and make sales.

Keep posting on Facebook. Run ads. Share content. But when someone clicks — send them to your website, not your Messenger inbox. That's where real business happens — with order tracking, payment confirmation, and proper customer records.

💡 Quick tip The most effective setup: Facebook and Instagram for marketing and awareness → Website for sales, bookings, and customer relationships. The two should work together — not replace each other.
